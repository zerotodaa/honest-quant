01

The trap

A leveraged crypto strategy backtested beautifully: 78 of 140 trades green, a clean equity curve, a healthy Sharpe. The out-of-sample split passed. The p-values looked significant. Everything a normal backtest checks said ship it.

It was a mirage. On a larger, truly independent sample of 438 trades, the same rules reversed to −$974. The edge was small-sample overfitting — and the standard guards were fooled right along with it.

02

Why it fooled the usual checks

  • Small-sample luck looks identical to skill until the sample gets big.
  • Multiple testing — try enough configs and the best one is noise wearing a Sharpe ratio.
  • One out-of-sample split isn't enough, and a raw Sharpe says nothing once you count how many things you tried.
03

What I build instead — an honest-evaluation gate

  • Nested cross-validation with a leakage guard — tune on inner folds, score once on untouched data.
  • Deflated & Probabilistic Sharpe — corrects for the number of configs tried and for fat tails.
  • Probability of Backtest Overfitting (PBO) via combinatorial CV.
  • A 300+ independent-sample gate — no edge is real until it survives one.
  • Real fills, slippage, funding and liquidation modeling — most engines silently skip funding and never liquidate, so they overstate every leveraged result.
04

Proof it isn't just theory

I rebuilt a Binance BTC perpetual strategy on a clean event-driven engine with this gate wired in. The verdict it returned: NO ROBUST EDGE AFTER DEFLATION — PBO 0.59, only 79 independent trades against the 300 gate. The gate blocked promotion to live. That is the system working: it refused to risk real money on a number that looked good and wasn't.
